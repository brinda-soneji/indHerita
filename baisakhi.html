Baisakhi

Baisakhi, also known as Vaisakhi, is a significant festival celebrated
primarily in the Punjab region of India, marking the start of the
harvest season. It falls on April 13th or 14th each year and holds
immense importance for both Sikhs and Hindus. For Sikhs, Baisakhi
commemorates the formation of the Khalsa in 1699 by Guru Gobind
Singh, which is celebrated with special prayers and processions
at Gurdwaras. Farmers rejoice in the successful harvest with vibrant
fairs, traditional dances like Bhangra and Gidda, and festive meals.
Baisakhi embodies the spirit of gratitude, community bonding, and the
celebration of nature's bounty.
